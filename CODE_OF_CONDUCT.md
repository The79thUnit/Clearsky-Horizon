# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

HORIZON is a public-health surveillance tool. We are committed to maintaining
a welcoming environment for contributors, researchers, journalists, and public
health professionals who want to improve it.

We pledge to make participation in this project a harassment-free experience
for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, sex
characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience,
education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race,
caste, colour, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

## Our Standards

**Expected behaviour:**

- Use welcoming and inclusive language
- Respect differing viewpoints and experiences
- Accept constructive criticism gracefully
- Focus on what is best for public health and the integrity of the data
- Show courtesy to other contributors and maintainers

**Unacceptable behaviour:**

- Harassment, trolling, or personal attacks in any form
- Publishing private information (doxxing) of any individual
- Deliberate submission of false or misleading epidemiological data
- Attempting to inflate case counts, manipulate source ratings, or corrupt
  the qualification pipeline for any purpose
- Any conduct that would bring disrepute to legitimate public health surveillance

## Data Integrity

Given the public-health mission of HORIZON, we hold data integrity to the
highest standard. Any attempt to submit fabricated case data, manipulate
confidence scores, or misrepresent source provenance will result in an
immediate and permanent ban and may be reported to relevant authorities.

## Enforcement

Instances of unacceptable behaviour may be reported to
**security@79thunit.co.uk**. All reports are reviewed promptly and treated
in confidence.

Maintainers have the right to remove, edit, or reject contributions that
violate this Code of Conduct, and to ban contributors for conduct they deem
harmful to the project or to public health.
